1. Intro


Learning isn't a skill often explicitly taught in school. So, people often think, "I'm just not smart enough," or "I can't remember things as well as that person - they must be gifted." And hey, I'm not going to lie, there's some truth to that. Factors like your educational background, your upbringing, how confident you are, and even a natural inclination towards certain topics can play a big part. But I truly believe there's a way for all of us to boost our productivity. It's all about understanding your personal learning cycles, especially when you're learning something big and new, like a language or coding.

Most people can relate to the feeling of being hyper-fixated on a topic, diving into it, learning a bunch, but then getting burnt out. That's when those Twitter gurus tell you it's about consistency and discipline. But wouldn't it be nice if we could stay motivated almost every day? üßê

2. Discovery


The concept of having a personal learning cycle definitely exists, but I only figured out mine after I began learning Mandarin and coding. I routinely get people telling me "you must be a fast learner!" or asking questions like "how do you plan your studies?". It got me thinking - what am I doing differently? So I decided to share my process.

With no experience learning Mandarin and only after learning for a about a year I had achieved a relatively high level of fluency compared to my peers, why? what am I doing differently that other people are not? same with coding, although I'm not sure how my speed compares to others, I still would like to share my process. Even if it helps just one other person I would be happy. üòÑ

3. Process


So here's the gist: I go through three main cycles when studying. Being aware of which cycle I'm in at any given moment has helped me stay productive without burning out. But keep in mind, for this process to work best, you have to actually enjoy what you're learning about. If it works for subjects you don't like as much, that's great, but no guarantees!

CYCLE 1

structure

This cycle refers to the time where a person is learning in a structured way.

Many developers can understand this cycle by thinking about learning on a platform like freeCodeCamp, Scrimba, or Codacademy. In regard to learning a language, this could be attending a language course, or following some kind of predeterimined learning path, like a textbook. During this process you will be introduced to fundemental concepts essential to the topic you are learning about.

How do you know when to move on to the next cycle? well, when you are feeling bored and totally demotivated. Feeling like your productivity is totally gone. You know you still like the topic but you have been met with a sense of burnout.

Then you move onto CYCLE 2

CYCLE 2

explore

The main purpose of this cycle is to inspire a new sense of motivation within yourself.

Imagine you have been in cycle 1 studying JavaScript for a week and feel totally tired. You must need a break, it will be quite difficult to regain motivation and energy from that passion of doing something you enjoy if you continue doing the very thing that has burnt you out. This method is related to a psychological concept called the ‚Äòpuppy mind‚Äô.

The "puppy mind" describes the tendency for the mind to get easily distracted and jump from one thing to another, much like a playful puppy. This can lead to difficulty in focusing on one task for an extended period of time.

people mostly agree that we need to train/discipline the puppy mind, and while I agree, there are some situations (like in cycle 2) that it might be beneficial to let your puppy mind run rampant.

The point of this cycle is to get a fresh perspective on learning, maximize your input sources to stimulate creativity.

CYCLE 3

create

This cycle is about creating something or making a project.

After the explore phase, you have hopefully encountered an idea for your CYCLE 3 project.

This idea may have come from seeing a problem you would like to solve, or something fun that you have seen. I have been inspired to make a project by various sources, maybe a challenge on front-end mentor, realizing my mom needed a new website, or one of the assigned projects on freeCodeCamp. Whether or not you are a creative person, there are plenty of resources to get inspired and make a project.

Very lovely response from chatGPT as you can see. This way, even if you haven't figured out a project on your own and you want to get into CYCLE 3 you can!

Once you are finished with your project you can move back to CYCLE 1 and learn more fundamentals and concepts.

Don't be afraid to switch learning resources if something is not clicking with you, and make things that you are passionate about. If I am doing something boring I can barely concentrate for 30 minutes, if I am passionate I can concentrate for many hours straight!

Happy learning!
