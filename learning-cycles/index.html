Learning is a process that isn’t specifically taught in schools. So people tend to think, oh im just not very smart or good at remembering things, that person is just gifted. And i think there is some truth to that, I wont lie to you, this could be a combination of a lot of reasons, previous education, childhood environment impact, confidence in oneself, and natural affinity towards specific topics. however i do think there is a way for everyone to become more productive, and that is about understanding the cycles that occur when you are learning something new. specifically a big topic, for ex a new language, or a coding language, or … Something we need to understand is our own personal learning cycles and the impact of passion and motivation, and how to inspire motivation. Most people can relate to the feeling of being hyperfixated on a topic at one point or another and totally diving into it learning a bunch but getting burnt out and demotivated at some point. THats when those motivation twitter gurus come in and say, “ oh its not about motivation its about being consistent and showing up every day” BUT wouldnt it be nice if you could be motivated? almost every day? The concepts of having a specific learning cycle defiently exists, however I only found out about it after I discovered my own cycle. So my cycle is slightly different and more applicable to coding or lanaguge learning specifically.
