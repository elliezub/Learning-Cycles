Learning isn't a skill often explicitly taught in school. So, people often think, "I'm just not smart enough," or "I can't remember things as well as that person - they must be gifted." And hey, I'm not going to lie, there's some truth to that. Factors like your educational background, your upbringing, how confident you are, and even a natural inclination towards certain topics can play a big part. But I truly believe there's a way for all of us to boost our productivity. It's all about understanding your personal learning cycles, especially when you're learning something big and new, like a language or coding.

Most people can relate to the feeling of being hyper-fixated on a topic, diving into it, learning a bunch, but then getting burnt out. That's when those Twitter gurus tell you it's about consistency and discipline. But wouldn't it be nice if we could stay motivated almost every day?

The concept of having a personal learning cycle definitely exists, but I only figured out mine after I began learning Mandarin and coding. My cycle seemed different, more effective. And it got me thinking - what am I doing differently? So I decided to share my process.

With no experience in mandarin and only after learning for a about a year I had achieved a relatively high level of fluency compared to my peers, why? what am i doing differently that other people are not? same with coding, although im not sure how my speed compares to others, I still would like to share my process. Even if it helps just one other person I would be happy

So here's the gist: I go through three main cycles when studying. Being aware of which cycle I'm in at any given moment has helped me stay productive without burning out. But keep in mind, for this process to work best, you have to actually enjoy what you're learning about. If it works for subjects you don't like as much, that's great, but no guarantees!

CYCLE 1

This cycle specifically refers to the time where a person is learning in a structured way, many developers can understand this by thinking about learning on a platform like freecodecamp, scrimba, or codeacademy. In regard to learning a language, this could be attending a language course, or following some kind of predeterimined path, like a textbook. During this process you will be introduced to fundemental concepts essential to the topic you are learning about.
