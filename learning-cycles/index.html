1. Intro


Learning isn't a skill often explicitly taught in school. So, people often think, "I'm just not smart enough," or "I can't remember things as well as that person - they must be gifted." And hey, I'm not going to lie, there's some truth to that. Factors like your educational background, your upbringing, how confident you are, and even a natural inclination towards certain topics can play a big part. But I truly believe there's a way for all of us to boost our productivity. It's all about understanding your personal learning cycles, especially when you're learning something big and new, like a language or coding.

Most people can relate to the feeling of being hyper-fixated on a topic, diving into it, learning a bunch, but then getting burnt out. That's when those Twitter gurus tell you it's about consistency and discipline. But wouldn't it be nice if we could stay motivated almost every day? üßê

3. Process


So here's the gist: I go through three main cycles when studying. Being aware of which cycle I'm in at any given moment has helped me stay productive without burning out. But keep in mind, for this process to work best, you have to actually enjoy what you're learning about. If it works for subjects you don't like as much, that's great, but no guarantees!

CYCLE 1

structure

This cycle refers to the time where a person is learning in a structured way.

Many developers can understand this cycle by thinking about learning on a platform like freeCodeCamp, Scrimba, or Codacademy. In regard to learning a language, this could be attending a language course, or following some kind of predeterimined path, like a textbook. During this process you will be introduced to fundemental concepts essential to the topic you are learning about.

Learning Mandarin example:

Keep in mind, if you attend a mandarin class, this CYCLE 1 will last for the entirety of the class, but you could add in some of the other cycles in your spare time.

This process is very simple, and the first time you are in the structure cycle you should be able to spend quite a while in it, depending on how many hours you spend a day learning you could be here for a few days up to a few weeks.

How do you know when to move on to the next cycle? well, when you are feeling bored and totally demotivated. Feeling like your productivity is totally gone. You know you still like the topic but you have been met with a sense of burnout.

Then you move onto CYCLE 2

CYCLE 2

explore

The main purpose of this cycle is to inspire a new sense of motivation within yourself.

Imagine you have been in cycle 1 studying JavaScript for a week and feel totally tired. You must need a break, it will be quite difficult to regain motivation and energy from that passion of doing something you enjoy if you continue doing the very thing that has burnt you out. This method is related to a psychological concept called the ‚Äòpuppy mind‚Äô.

The "puppy mind" is a concept used in psychology to describe the tendency for the mind to get easily distracted and jump from one thing to another, much like a playful puppy. This can lead to difficulty in focusing on one task for an extended period of time.

people mostly agree that we need to train the puppy mind, and while I agree, there are some situations (like in cycle 2) that it might be beneficial to let your puppy mind run rampant.

The point of this cycle is to get a fresh perspective on learning, maximize your input sources to stimulate creativity.
